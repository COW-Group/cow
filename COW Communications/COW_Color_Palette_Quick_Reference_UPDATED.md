# COW Color Palette Quick Reference

## Philosophy
**"The Horizon: Cerulean sky meets earth. Never floating alone."**

Our signature aesthetic balances aspiration with foundation. Every design shows this relationship—cerulean (sky/water) grounded by earth tones (stone/clay/growth).

## Usage Rules

### ✅ DO:
- Always include cerulean (even if just an accent)
- Always ground with earth tone (never cerulean floating alone)
- Use 60-30-10 color ratio
- Create horizon moments (where colors meet)
- Consider warmth vs. cool balance

### ❌ DON'T:
- Use cerulean on white only (too cold, ungrounded)
- Use earth tones without cerulean (loses brand identity)
- Mix too many earth tones (muddy, unclear)
- Forget gold for special moments
- Use colors without purpose

---

## Testing Your Colors

**Before finalizing any design, check:**

1. **Brand Test:** Is cerulean visible and recognizable?
2. **Grounding Test:** Is there earth-tone foundation?
3. **Balance Test:** Does it feel warm AND professional?
4. **Contrast Test:** Does it meet WCAG AAA standards?
5. **Mood Test:** Does it feel grounded yet aspirational?

## Accessibility Standards

### Text Contrast Requirements:

**On White (#FFFFFF):**
- ✅ Deep Cerulean (#007BA7) - 5.6:1 (AA Large)
- ✅ Ink Black (#2C3E50) - 12.6:1 (AAA)
- ⚠️ Light Cerulean (#4FC3E0) - 2.8:1 (Decorative only)
- ⚠️ Warm Stone (#9B8B7E) - 3.2:1 (Decorative only)

**On Rice Paper (#F5F3F0):**
- ✅ Ink Black (#2C3E50) - 11.8:1 (AAA)
- ✅ Deep Cerulean (#007BA7) - 5.2:1 (AA Large)
- ⚠️ Bamboo Green (#6B8E6F) - 3.5:1 (Large text only)

**Key Rule:** Body text must use Ink Black or Deep Cerulean on white/rice paper backgrounds.
